When someone invites you to speak for 15 minutes, they are asking for more than 15 minutes of your time. In fact, Woodrow Wilson once suggested that the duration of time needed for preparation is inversely proportionate to that for which you are to speak:

If I am to speak ten minutes, I need a week for preparation; if fifteen minutes, three days; if half an hour, two days; if an hour, I am ready now. 1

I don’t think my distribution of time needed would look like Wilson’s. I certainly wouldn’t feel ready to give an hour-long talk right now. But the point is that speaking requires quite a bit of time.2

It’s definitely worth it.3 I am incredibly grateful for the opportunities I’ve had to speak, and those who have helped and encouraged me along the way.

At the same time, talks and posters are a huge part of what conferences are made of – they’re “the show.” So, it should basically be mutually beneficial.

All of this was inspired by the Call for Speakers for Women in Analytics 2020, which includes a list of Benefits of Speaking at WIA.4
